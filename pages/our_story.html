Founded

A Dream Born in the Kitchen

It all started with Sarah Wanjiku's grandmother's recipe book and a tiny kitchen in Westlands. After years working in corporate Nairobi, Sarah discovered her true calling when friends couldn't stop talking about her weekend baking experiments.

"I realized that baking wasn't just about creating something delicious," Sarah recalls. "It was about creating moments of joy, bringing people together, and preserving the art of handcrafted food in our fast-paced world."

What began as weekend treats for neighbors has grown into Nairobi's most beloved artisanal bakery, but the heart remains the same - every creation is made with love, intention, and respect for tradition.

Sarah Wanjiku

Founder & Head Baker

Our Values

The principles that guide every decision, from ingredient sourcing to community involvement

Local Partnerships

We source coffee from Kenyan roasters, work with local organic farms, and support community suppliers who share our commitment to quality.

Sustainability

Eco-friendly packaging, minimal waste practices, and energy-efficient equipment reflect our commitment to environmental responsibility.

Community First

From sponsoring local events to providing baking workshops, we believe in giving back to the Westlands community that supports us.

Our Baking Philosophy

Time & Patience

Our sourdough ferments for 72 hours, allowing complex flavors to develop naturally. Good things take time, and we never rush the process.

Seasonal Ingredients

We rotate our menu based on seasonal availability, ensuring peak freshness and supporting local agricultural cycles.

Handcrafted Excellence

Every item receives individual attention. From hand-shaping bread to personally decorating each cake, craftsmanship is our signature.
